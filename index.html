Our government has failed this nation's heroes.
Did you know?
That 20 veterans commit suicide every day.
That's 600 every month and 7200 every year.
We are ignoring the hard facts.
In 2008 our government sponsored a Rand Study to analyze the mental health of post deployed veterans. 1,965 veterans where surveyed and analyzed in total.

14% of those veterans analyzed where diagnosed with Post Traumatic Stress Disorder, and 14% of those where diagnosed with Depression. Additionally, 9% of the veterans where diagnosed with both PTSD and depression.

About the book :

These numbers are shocking, but what's even more shocking is the notion that our government has prescribed generic brand anti-depressants to our soldiers in-place of proven name brand anti-depressants. This is unacceptable, our soldiers are putting their lives on the line for our freedom and we are knowingly compromising their mental health by not supplying them with the brand certified tools they need to facilitate a healthy mental state.

It's outrageous that we are losing 20 soldiers a day to suicide on our own soil because they are not being denied the proper mental health treatment. War is traumatic for all, and our government is not giving our soldiers the health care they deserve.

Too many lives are being lost each year to PTSD and depression. This is especially alarming because these illnesses are very treat- able with several medications that are proven to be effective. So why do the suicide rates persist?

PTSD is treatable, that’s right treatable. 14 recent scientific studies have demonstrated that with the brand-name serotonin reuptake inhibitors (medication). These are brand Prozac, brand Paxil, brand Celexa, brand Luvox and brand Zoloft.

The base pharmacies, which the veterans also use do not stock the brand medications on the generic medications, fluoxetine, fluoxetine, paroxetine, citalopram, sertraline. These generic medications have never been tested in scientific studies for their effectiveness in treating PTSD. I stopped using them in my practice shortly after they came out because there were not effective in treating my patients with PTSD and depression. The solution to this problem is simple. Stock the base and VA pharmacies with the brand medications. Brand Prozac, brand Paxil, brand Celexa, brand Luvox and brand Zoloft. The suicide rates would wall drastically and PTSD would not develop into chronic PTSD that lasts a lifetime.

About the author :

Herbert Wagemaker is a board-certified psychiatrist.

He has a lifelong passion for helping people who suffer from mental illness. He has extensively researched PTSD and successfully helped numerous people achieve and maintain functionality in their daily lives. In this book he shares crucial information that explains why some current treatment options just do not work and most importantly, which ones do.

Dr. Wagemaker taught at the University of Louisville from 1975 to 1986 during which time his research in the causes of schizophrenia was funded by the National Institute of Mental Health. He was also involved in psychopharmacology research. He later joined the faculty of the Department of Psychiatry at the University of Florida’s teaching hospital, Shands Jacksonville, where he was director of inpatient psychiatry.
